The Problem

Your buddy swears creatine fixed his brain fog. Reddit says ashwagandha is life-changing. Some influencer claims cold plunges cured her depression.

You try it. Maybe something happens, maybe placebo... It's hard to know when your sample size is one.

What if 50-100 people ran the same protocol, pulled the same bloodwork before and after, and published everything?

That's the play. Real interventions. Real biomarkers. Shared data. No sponsor influence and no broken telephone. Just answers.

How It Works

Simple process, rigorous output
01

We Choose a Protocol

One intervention. Clear biomarkers. Something safe, accessible, and actually worth testing. Community votes on what's next.

02

You Run It

Baseline labs. 30 days on protocol. Weekly check-ins with the cohort. Follow-up labs. We keep each other accountable.

03

We Publish Everything

Aggregated results. Anonymized individual data. Honest breakdown of what moved and what didn't. No spin.

Principles

Transparency

Protocols, raw data, and analysis are public. We show our work—including when results are boring.

No Hype

Safety First

We test interventions with established safety profiles. Curiosity yes, recklessness no.

Community-Run

Participants decide what we test. The experiments belong to everyone who runs them.
